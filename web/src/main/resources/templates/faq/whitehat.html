About Cindercloud Whitehat

Why are we sweeping addresses?
Our application is constantly expanding its database with transactions, blocks, addresses and statistics of the Ethereum blockchain.
We noticed that quite some accounts got compromised and funds were being stolen on a daily basis.

Because we have a lot of data on the blockchain, we're sometimes able to figure out how accounts were compromised. Having this information, it happens that we're able to have access to these credentials. As a security measure, we're actively sweeping these accounts trying to be faster than malicious actors.
How are we sweeping those addresses?
First of all we're constantly checking every address that we have credentials to in our database to see if they have balance. If one does, we initiate a transaction to one of our own accounts.


Our second modus operandi to try and beat malicious actors is to check pending and fresh transactions to see if one of our compromised accounts will receive or just received a balance. If it does, we sweep the account.
Is this stealing?
We're not stealing your funds. We're sweeping them to make sure no hacker can steal them from you. If you're able to prove it was your account in the first place, we'll be transferring them to a secure wallet.
What happens to these sweeped funds?
Sweeped funds are being stored in cold storage. If a user has proven ownership of one of the compromised accounts - which can sometimes take some time - he will be redeemed the sweeped funds.

Sometimes funds will be moved around to a new cold storage, but there's nothing to worry, we'll never sell your coins and will always have the equivalent of the stolen funds in cold storage.

Redeeming stolen funds

How can know it was Cindercloud that sweeped my funds?
At the moment, we're working on a page where a user can enter his address and email and we'll investigate if cindercloud sweeped the funds. However, as this is not available yet, we urge you to comment on the cindercloud address disqus page or on etherscan.

You can always contact us on twitter or by shooting us an email too.
Why and how can I prove that I was the original owner?
First of all, after we've checked that it was us who sweeped your account, we'll be asking a few questions depending on the history of your account. If another account originally sent you funds, we'll ask for a signed message from that address or a verification transactions to our account with 0 eth.
If funds originated from an exchange, we'll need proof from the exchange that it was you who initiated the transaction.
Why so many verification steps?
We first started with way less security measures, but after a few days, it because quite clear that some people just tried to act as if it was their compromised address, effectively trying to scam us.

The only way we can reimburse the original owner is by knowing 100% sure that it were his funds. If it's really you, I'm sure you'll be able to prove how you acquired the funds.
